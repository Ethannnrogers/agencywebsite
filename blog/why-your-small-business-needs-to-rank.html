Why Your Small Business Needs to Rank on Google

If you own or operate a local service business, there are literally hundreds of people in your area searching for the very services you provide. Although, when customers are searching for services, without having a properly optimized online presence, Google suggests they use your competitors.

Here are the numbers

  • Over 42% of searching customers click on the first listing
  • 83% of consumers do a search before making a transaction
  • Less than 8% of searching customers go past the 4th listing


Google Maps Ranking

Google Maps is usually the first thing to show up in a search result and if you aren’t ranking well, it is almost impossible for new customers to find your business. When consumers are searching for services in your area, they don’t have time and aren’t interested in comparing every single listing available. They are simply going to look at the first few businesses, and decide who to call. If you don’t have a good ranking, no one is going to call.

A good ranking is determined by a variety of different things. Accurate business information (Address, Hours of Operation, Website Link, Phone Number), business photos, and customer reviews are what determines how your business listing will rank. Once you can rank against competitors, it is mostly up to your photos and reviews that makes people want to do business with you or not.



Local 3-Pack


When someone is searching for local businesses, google shows the top 3 ranked Google My Business listings before any other web links.

These top 3 listings are known as the local 3-pack. These three listings are a stripped down version of the listing making it faster to read necessary information. The information includes the business name, overall rating, business category, address, phone number, and operating hours of that day.

It is highly important to get your Google My Business listing into the top 3. Over 70% of web searchers don't click past the first 3 listings. There are thousands of people searching for the services that your business offers and if you aren’t ranking well on Google, you’re missing all the opportunity to turn them into customers.
